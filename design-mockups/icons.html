MasterControl — icon studies

Six directions inspired by the original 1982 Tron. Each renders well at 32px and 1024px. Single-color silhouettes pair best with the existing Verdant / Pine / Carbon themes; the red/cyan dual-tone variants are loudest.

32 / 48 / 64
1 · MCP Face
Rebuilt to match the reference: flat-top helmet with a chevron chin point, big almond eye sockets with tiny white pupils in the upper-inner corner (gaze looking up and out), heavy brow band, two vertical nostril slits, and an unmistakably arched-down frown.
2 · MCP Pillar
Full-body MCP — the cylindrical tower with stacked light rings, two eye-slots near the top. Most narrative of the bunch. Reads at small sizes as a vertical red form.
3 · Identity Disc
The throwable disc. Most icon-like silhouette — a bullseye with circuit gaps. Cleanest at small sizes. No face, but unmistakably Tron.
4 · Grid Horizon
Vanishing-point Tron grid with a rising red sun. Atmospheric and unmistakable. The most "scenic" of the set — gives MasterControl a cinematic identity.
5 · M Circuit
Bold "M" monogram with circuit-trace tails clipping out of the frame. Most app-icon-conventional — reads instantly as a brand mark, less narrative.
6 · Recognizer
The four-legged "Recognizer" walker, simplified to a U-shape on two pillars with a single eye-slot. Distinctive silhouette, deeply Tron — and reads as a stylized arch / portal at small sizes.
